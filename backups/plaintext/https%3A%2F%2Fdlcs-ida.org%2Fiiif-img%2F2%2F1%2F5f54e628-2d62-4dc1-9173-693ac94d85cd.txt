The large general hospital at the U. S. Indian School here
has continued to serve the neighboring pueblos well during the
year, both medically and surgically. As many cases as possible
of trachoma, tuberculosis, and venereal disease are hospitalized.
Temporary tents are not needed.
Seventeen cases of venereal disease were reported, many of
which were hospitalized. Hospitalization appears to be the most
practical way of waging any campaign against venereal disease.
