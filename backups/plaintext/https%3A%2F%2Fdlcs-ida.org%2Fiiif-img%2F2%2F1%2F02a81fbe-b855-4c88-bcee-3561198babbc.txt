Allotments, etc.It is impossible for our Indians to live wholly upontheir allotments as this would not afford them sufficientgrazing land to take care of their stock. Also, many ofthe allotments do not have any water developed on them andit is necessary for the Nava joes to move constantly inorder to secure feed and water for their sheep and cattle.They make use of all allotted and unallotted land und erGovernment control, together with additional acreage ownedby outsiders, which they are allowed to use.4.
