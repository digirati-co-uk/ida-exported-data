September 26, 1912.- 24Pueblo Day Sohools, Albuquerque.NARRATIVE.Seotion VI----- Field Irrigation.The water used by the various Indians in thepueblos has been secured by appropriation and use for centuries.They have been using sufficient water to insure the irrigationof all their irrigable land.The San Felipe, Sandia, SantaAna and Isleta supply comes from the Rio Grande in ditohes constructed by themselves, though the head gates are in no instancepermanent, necessitating work each spring by the Indians. This,however, is done in a very cheerful and satisfactory mannerunder the direction of the pueblo officials. The source of supply for the Laguna and Acoma pueblos is the San Jose river andmountain streams from Mt. Taylor.
