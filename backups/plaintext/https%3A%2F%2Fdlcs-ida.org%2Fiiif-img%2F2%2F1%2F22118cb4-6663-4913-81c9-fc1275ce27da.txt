U.S. Indian Industrial School,
Santa Fe, New Mexico October 19, 1903.
T.P.Martin M.D.,
Taos, New Mexico,
Sir:
I am in receipt of your letter of the 15th instant in which you
inolose a letter frou Mrs.Hayes the teacher at Piouri. Pueblo, in re
gard to diphtheria.
I beg to state that I do not think it absoluta
ly necessary to secure a quantity of antitoxine unless the danger
is great.
Of course should the diphtheria appear in the Pueblo every
effort will be made to eradicate it.
Very respectfully,
deglamenco
Superintendents
